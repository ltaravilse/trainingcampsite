History of the Training Camp

The idea of organizing a Training Camp came up in 2010 during the ACM ICPC World Finals when we realized that the teams from Argentina that qualified to the finals were always from the same universities. That year, Nicolás Álvarez, Fidel Schaposnik and Leopoldo Taravilse started talking about the good results the Russian teams had after going to their Training Camps, and decided to do one in Argentina to train teams from all over the country.

That year, in August, we organized the first edition of our Training Camp at Universidad de Buenos Aires. That year the Training Camp was coordinated by Nicolás Álvarez, it lasted a week and it had around 25 students, all of them from universities in Argentina.

We had such an impact that in the second edition, in 2011, when we decided to repeat the experience, we received students from Bolivia and Peru, besides the teams from Argentina that participated in that Training Camp.

In 2012 we took two important decisions. The first of them was to start rotating the site, so we could reach students from all over the country taking them the Training Camp to their universities. The second one, was start doing two weeks Training Camps. Since 2012 all the editions were of two weeks, and this decision was made in part because of the feedback from the community. That year we received almost 100 students from different universities in Argentina, Bolivia, Chile, Peru and Uruguay.

Since 2012 Training Camps were hosted in Córdoba, La Plata, Buenos Aires, Bahía Blanca, Neuquén, and in 2017 we'll have the VIII edition at Universidad Nacional de San Martín. In the last years we already started receiving students from Colombian and Venezuelan universities.
